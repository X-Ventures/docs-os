## Supply Dynamics

TAO follows a Bitcoin-like halving schedule with a 21M hard cap.

| Phase | Period | Daily Emission | Notes |
|-------|--------|---------------|-------|
| Current | 2024–2026 | ~7,200 TAO/day | Pre-first-halving |
| Post-Halving 1 | ~2026 | ~3,600 TAO/day | Emission rate halves |
| Post-Halving 2 | ~2030 | ~1,800 TAO/day | Scarcity increases |

Current supply is 46% of max (9.60M / 21M). Early stakers benefit from higher emissions before halvings reduce the rate.

## Market Thesis

### Bull Case

- AI/crypto convergence is the defining narrative of 2025–2027
- Bittensor is the leading decentralized AI protocol by market cap and developer activity
- TAO is 74% below ATH — significant upside if narrative returns
- Validator yield + price appreciation = compounding returns
- Halving schedule creates supply scarcity

### Bear Case

- TAO price could decline further (crypto winter scenario)
- Subnet quality is uneven — many subnets produce low-value outputs
- Regulatory risk for staking-as-a-service classification
- Technical complexity and operational burden
- Weight copying commoditizes validation, compressing yields

### Base Case

- TAO consolidates in $150–$300 range near-term
- Validator yields remain 4–6% APY
- Network matures, top subnets produce real AI value
- Early validators build delegation moats
